5. Case Scenarios

Apply learning through interactive scenario questions.

Behavioural Disturbance: Assess risk, monitor airway/breathing/circulation, and screen for hypoxia, hypoglycaemia, intoxication, trauma, and delirium.
De-escalation: One lead communicator, calm tone, clear boundaries, reduced stimuli, and maintain egress for crew safety.
Sedation & MOA: Review mechanism, indication, and adverse effects before administration. Plan for immediate airway support and ventilatory rescue.
NSW Mental Health Act: Use the relevant legal authority for safe apprehension/transport decisions and document your rationale clearly.
Case Scenarios: Practice what to do next under pressure and debrief why safer options are preferred.
