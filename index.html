Pathophysiology points

  1. DVT Formation (Virchow's Triad): venous stasis, endothelial injury, and hypercoagulability promote thrombus formation.
  2. Embolization: part of the thrombus detaches and becomes an embolus.
  3. PE Effects: obstruction in pulmonary vessels impairs gas exchange, raises pulmonary vascular resistance, and can strain the right ventricle.
