Behavioural Disturbance & Mental Health Crisis Learning Tool

Interactive training for paramedics using NSW Ambulance and Queensland CPG principles. Build confidence in de-escalation, legal decision-making, and safe patient transport.

De-escalation Techniques

Summary: Prioritise scene safety, calm communication, and collaborative options before restrictive interventions.

Verbal strategies

  • Use a calm tone, short sentences, and non-threatening body language.
  • Validate emotion without endorsing unsafe behaviour: “I can see you’re distressed.”
  • Offer choices to restore control: “Would you prefer to sit outside or in the ambulance?”

Communication examples

“My role is to keep everyone safe. Tell me what you need right now so we can make a plan together.”

Deep dive: Consider stimulus reduction, role assignment, and using one lead communicator.

Treatment & Medications (Behavioural Disturbance)

Summary: Assess reversible causes first (e.g., hypoxia, hypoglycaemia, intoxication, head injury), then escalate treatment proportionately.

  • Continuous risk assessment: patient, crew, bystanders.
  • Use least restrictive option likely to succeed.
  • If sedation required, monitor airway, breathing, circulation, and sedation depth closely.

Deep dive: Align medication choice, dose pathway, and monitoring with current local NSW Ambulance/QLD CPG editions and service policy.

Legal Framework (NSW Mental Health Act)

Summary: Legal authority supports safe assessment, transport, and coordinated care where mental illness or disturbance creates significant risk.

Key sections to know

  • Section 20: Paramedic powers relating to persons believed to require assessment/care.
  • Section 22: Police powers to apprehend and transport where criteria are met.
  • Section 81: Transport/restraint considerations and lawful handling safeguards.

Deep dive: Document rationale, risk indicators, and least restrictive decisions clearly.

Interactive Scenarios

Summary: Practice structured decisions in realistic cases with immediate feedback.

  • “What would you do next?” branching prompts.
  • Feedback explains why an option is safer or higher risk.
  • Designed for classroom discussion or self-paced LMS review.
